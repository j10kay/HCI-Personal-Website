11,000+ Scientists Warn That The World Is in a 'Climate Emergency'

A group of 11,258 scientists from 153 countries issued a clear, alarming message on November 5, 2019: the planet faces a climate crisis that can only be avoided by a global shift to more eco-conscious policies.

The study, titled “World Scientists’ Warning of a Climate Emergency,” lists six policy goals that the authors believe must be met to address the emergency: the global implementation of massive energy efficiency and conservation practices; switching to renewable energy sources in place of fossil fuels; committing to an end to burning any remaining coal and oil to generate energy; and adopting a mostly plant-based diet.

STUDY: People of Color Face Severe Racial Disparities in American Health Care

A troubling report on racial inequities in the U.S. healthcare system was released December 19, 2019 by The Century Foundation. The study, “Racism, Inequality, and Health Care for African-Americans,” shows that this inequality disproportionately affects people of color and other marginalized communities, but African Americans specifically “bear the brunt of the nation’s healthcare challenges.”

These are key findings from the report:

  1. There is less coverage for African Americans.
  2. Black households spend more on healthcare.
  3. African-American infant mortality rates are higher.
  4. Racism negatively impacts African Americans’ health.

These public option plans fail to provide the high-quality care that African Americans require.

Technology

Apple fined $27 million in France

France’s competition watchdog DGCCRF recently announced that Apple will pay a $27.4 million fine due to a questionable iOS update.

Some years ago, Apple released an iOS update that introduced a new performance-capping feature for older devices. This resulted in the abrupt shutdown of phones. Many users noticed that their phones got slower but didn’t know that replacing the battery would fix that, occasionally forcing them to – needlessly – buy new phones.

Given that iPhone users can’t downgrade to a previous iOS version, they had no choice. Apple has accepted the fine, recognizing its wrongdoing with a statement on its website.

Study Says Automation Will Deepen Work Shortage for Black Men

Research shows that the racial wealth gap between Black and White families continues to grow, the average White family holds over ten times the wealth of the average Black family. A report published by McKinsey & Company says that the increase in companies adopting automation only makes matters worse.

“The Future of Work in Black America” projects that many Black people, especially men, will have a harder time finding work as the rise of AI threatens to replace working class jobs. By 2030, Black men between ages 18 and 35 who don’t have a college degree will be hit hardest.
